Article 1

Objects of the contract

Whereas the Supplier is responsible for the importation and distribution of petroleum and petroleum products.

Whereas the buyer hereby is desirous to enter into a contractual deal for the purchase of petroleum and petroleum products for its Factory located at {{ contract.location }}, {{ contract.location1 }} subject to the terms and conditions of this contract dated on {{ contract.contract_date }}.

Now therefore, the parties have entered into this agreement according to the following terms and conditions.

Article 2

Definitions

Unless the context otherwise requires

a. "Product" means Gasoil specified in the proforma.


b. "Government" means the Government of the Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia.

c. "Force Majeure" means Natural unforeseen disaster acts of /God/ (including fire, flood, earthquake, storm, hurricane, or other natural disaster), war, invasion, act of foreign enemies, hostilities (regardless of whether war is declared) civil war, rebellion, revolution, insurrection, military or usurped power or terrorist activities, nationalization, government sanction, blockage, embargo, strike, lockout or interruption or failure of electricity service or other inevitable or unforeseen causes.



Article 3

Exclusive Supplier

During the contract period the Buyer is obligated to purchase exclusively from the Buyer all the product mentioned under Annex A.


Article 4

Supply of Products

Delivery of the product sold under this agreement shall be made as specified in
the proforma or at the place both parties jointly agreed in written.


Article 5

Product Delivery Schedule፣ transport

5.1 The buyer will provide details of product order to be made for the coming month on the 15th day of the current month on a weekly basis. This will state the total product required to be delivered at the delivery place mentioned under this contract.


5.2 Upon receiving the details of the order, the supplier as much as possible delivers the products. But the delay to deliver on time does not allow the buyer to claim compensation


5.3 The Supplier shall ensure delivery as per the order of the buyer stated above in Art.


5.4 The buyer is obliged to purchase {{ contract.obliged_purchase }} trucks fuel and {{ contract.amount_litter }} litters of lubricants in a month/ demand based lubricants.


5.5 The supplier will provide transportation service for Addis Ababa and its surroundings if the buyer makes order for lubricants and greases items of 3,120 litters or above. But if it is out of Addis Ababa the buyer will provide transportation cost.
5.6 The risk attached to and the title of all the products sold shall be transferred to the Purchaser upon delivery thereof in accordance with the attached proforma.

Article 6

Weights and Measurements

The Supplier's weights and measurement as verified and approved by the official body of the Government shall be accepted as conclusive evidence of the quantity of fuel comprising each delivery, but the buyer shall appoint at his expense a representative at the place of the transfer of possession, check such weights and measurement. In case of discrepancy at the delivery point, the buyer must ensure, before discharge into storage tanks, that the Supplier's driver agrees and signs on the invoice for quantity short delivered.

Article 7

Resale of the products

Under this Contract, the Supplier sells the products to the Buyer, and the Buyer shall not re-sell the products nor assign its rights under this Contract without the written consent of the Seller.

Article 8

Price of the Product

The products to be purchased under the terms of this agreement and their prices as specified in the proforma are subject to adjustment upward or downward in the event of changes made by the government.
Therefore, the price of the fuel shall be set as per the loading date price from Djibouti, Sudan, and Government's Depots. The price covered by this agreement is based on the Supplier's normal source and line of supply, but in the event that the Supplier is obliged either by action of the responsible bodies or through circumstances beyond his control to take products from other sources or by other lines of supply, the supplier reserves the right to adjust prices accordingly to cover all additional supply costs upon prior notification of the knowledge of the events.

Article 9

Terms of Payment

9.1. The buyer shall make the full payment before placing an order for the products.

9.2 Where the buyer fails to make advance payment, the Supplier shall have the right to suspend further deliveries until payment in full is made.

Article 10

Technical Support

10.1 The Supplier shall provide all the necessary technical advice and training on the fuel application to the Purchaser's technical staff. 10.2 The Supplier shall provide training on lubricants, safety, the use of quality checking tools mentioned above and product receiving procedures.

Article 11

Force Majeure

By reason of force majeure, the supplier shall be at liberty to withhold, suspend, or reduce deliveries under this agreement to any extent the buyer shall then be free to purchase from other suppliers on its account any deficiencies hereunder caused by the operation of this clause and only for the duration of its operation.

Article 12

Fuel Receipt Procedure

The buyer shall follow strictly the procedure for the discharging of the fuel.

Article 13

Demurrage charge

Under normal operation, the buyer shall pay delivery Delay for the delay beyond 3 (three) working hours to discharge at the delivery point at the rate of Birr 250.00 per hour. According to the current relevant law, working hours are between 8:30 am and 5:30 pm while the lunch break is between 12:30 and 01:30 pm on any working days in the year excluding official time-offs.
The time of arrival of the vehicle and the time of completion of the discharge of the product shall be registered, signed, and sealed by the Purchaser and counter- signed by the driver of the vehicle on the supplier's invoice which shall be the basis for the claim.

Article 14

Duration and Termination of the Agreement

14.1 This Agreement shall be valid for {{ contract.valid_year }} years starting from the signing of this contract. If either party needs to terminate the contract before the contract period lapsed, the party who needs the termination needs to give a minimum of 2 (two) months written notice to the other party of its intention to terminate the agreement.

14.2 Notwithstanding early termination, each party shall fulfill all obligations accrued under this Agreement prior to the time such termination becomes effective.
14.3 Termination effected by a party under this Article shall not affect any other rights or remedies of such party under the law or otherwise.

Article 15

Amendments

This agreement shall be modified or superseded by mutual consent of the contracting parties in written form only.

Article 16

Confidentiality

The information contained under this Contract and all information exchanged relating to it are confidential between the buyer and the Supplier.

Article 17

Liability

Except where expressly provided in this agreement, neither the Supplier nor the customer shall be liable for consequential, indirect, or special damages arising out of or in connection with this agreement.

Article 18

Consequential Loss

18.1 If there is a shortage of fuel from the original source, the Seller will notify the Buyer of it as soon as he gets the information.
18.2 Neither party shall have any liability to the other in respect of any breach of this Contract for any loss of business profit, revenue, missed contractual opportunity, or any direct or consequential loss, however, it arises.

Article 19

Jurisdiction and Settlement of Disputes

This agreement shall be construed and take effect in accordance with the laws in force in Ethiopia, and the parties hereby consent to the jurisdiction of the Ethiopian Court.
If any dispute arises from or in connection with this agreement, the parties shall make all efforts to settle amicably, and failing that, either party may resort to legal action in the Federal Court found in Addis Ababa.

Notice
